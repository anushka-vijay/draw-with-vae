1 · DRAW
z = μ+σε —
z = μ + σ · ε, ε ~ N(0, I)
Reparameterization trick: ε is sampled separately so gradients can flow through μ and σ during backprop.
σ (spread) —
q(z|x) = N(μ(x), σ²(x))
The encoder outputs a distribution, not a point. Rings on the map show 1σ and 2σ confidence ellipses.
2 · LATENT SPACE
hover the map to decode
KL loss —
KL = −½ Σ(1 + log σ² − μ² − σ²)
Measures how far the encoder's distribution drifts from N(0,1). This term organizes the latent clusters.
Recon loss —
ℒ_recon = −Σ[x·log x̂ + (1−x)·log(1−x̂)]
Binary cross-entropy per pixel — how different is the reconstruction from your original drawing?
Total loss —
ℒ = ℒ_recon + β · KL(q(z|x) ‖ p(z))
Good reconstructions, penalized by how far the encoder drifts from N(0,1). β controls that trade-off.
β 1
β ∈ {0,1} → ℒ = ℒ_recon + β·KL
β=0 drops the KL term entirely. The encoder scatters clusters anywhere — no organization enforced. Toggle and watch the map.
